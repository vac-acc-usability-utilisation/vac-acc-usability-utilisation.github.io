URL Mapping

Use case: Slightly modifying content for a better sign-in and registration flow

The problem: Our registration and sign-in flow is complex. Due to the nature of third-party sign-in services we can't tell if a person is going to the sign-in or registration process until after they have logged in through a sign-in partner. That makes it difficult to present a page with clear content and contextual help.

How it impacts users: Having both a 'sign-in' and 'register' button is a common web design pattern that users expect. It's confusing to arrive on a page and read "sign-in or register" or "authentication methods".

A solution: What we can do is leverage WET-BOEW's URLMapping to help us tweak the content of the heading, lead, and buttons

  1. From the previous page with Register and Sign-in buttons (veterans.gc.ca / https://www.canada.ca/en/services/veterans.html) we append each button's link like so:
<!-- link to MVA -->/sign-in-services.html?register
---
or
---
<!-- link to MVA -->/sign-in-services.html?sign-in
  1. Now when the user arrives we can modify the content to suit their intent. For instance, by default the first heading on the page will read
    • "Veteran Affairs Canada sign in and registration services", or if the user came from a sign-in button it will read "Veteran Affairs Canada sign in services",
