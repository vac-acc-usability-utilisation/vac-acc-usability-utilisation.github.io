Virtual Medical Assessment preparation

Virtual medical assessment
  • A virtual assessment is available for your Post-traumatic Stress Disorder.
  • Or, your medical provider can provide the assessment. You also have the option of an in-person assessment when our offices reopen.
Technical requirements (required)

The virtual assessment uses Microsoft Teams videoconferencing software.

Due to firewall restrictions, the virtual assessment cannot be joined from the Canadian Armed Forces network.

Physical space requirements (required)
  • The doctor may need to see the affected body part and how you move certain parts of your body to assess your condition.
  • Depending on your disability, you may need to undress, and you will need a big enough space for this assessment.
  • Ensure the space is setup in a way in which you can point the camera at the affected body parts.
  • Ensure you are in a private and quiet space for the session.
  • Please relocate pets to another room.
Privacy and disclosing information (required)

Details of your medical history and examination results will be discussed.

Veterans Affairs Canada shall not record videos, audio, or take photos during the virtual assessment.

All persons physically present in the room during the assessment must be disclosed to the doctor and to you.

A chaperone may be present at your request or at the request of a doctor.
