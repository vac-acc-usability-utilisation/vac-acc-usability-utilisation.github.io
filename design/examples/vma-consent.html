Meeting platform

The assessment will be conducted on Microsoft Teams, a videoconferencing platform.

Potential impact on benefits

If the assessment confirms that your condition has worsened, your disability benefit, will be adjusted accordingly — unless you are already receiving the maximum amount payable of 100% assessment.

Right to have a chaperone

You have the right to a chaperone during the virtual medical assessment.

You may:

  • ask VAC to provide a chaperone for the virtual medical assessment
  • stop the virtual medical assessment at any time to ask questions about what the Medical Officer is doing
  • stop the virtual medical assessment and ask for a chaperone, even if you refused one at first

If you have questions or feel uncomfortable with a part of the assessment, please ask the Medical Officer to stop and tell them about your concerns.

Information disclosure

You may need to disclose personal and medical information and attendance details during the virtual medical assessment. This information is crucial for an accurate evaluation of your condition.

Consent acknowledgment

By clicking Continue, you acknowledge that you have read and understand the virtual medical assessment consent information above. This acknowledgment signifies your agreement to participate in the reassessment process.

Cancel your virtual medical assessment

You are about to cancel your virtual medical assessment. You are still entitled to your reassessment through one of our other options. Once you cancel, please contact VAC to reschedule your reassessment.
