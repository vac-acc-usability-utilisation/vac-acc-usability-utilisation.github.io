Slips and mistakes

Changing the behaviour of My VAC Account's sign out button

Errors are unavoidable.

Human errors are distinguished between slips and mistakes.

Slips

Slips are when we make an error executing a plan.

They are errors like sending a message to the wrong person or using a tablespoon rather than a teaspoon. Doing something you did not intend to.

These are slips in attention. We're in auto-pilot mode, and people are distracted by their environment and thoughts.

Slips happen to experts more often than beginners.

Preventing slips:

  • Adjust the design depending on the type of slip.
  • If people need essential information, we design to direct attention to important details.
  • Allow people to recover.

Mistakes

Mistakes are when we make an error in developing a plan.

They are decisions made on incorrect information. Doing something you intended that turns out to be wrong.

Errors aren't about the person making the mistakes, and they're about human limitations in cognitive load and a person's environment. We cannot convince people to think a specific way so that they use an interface the way we assume they should. We must understand what they're trying to achieve and meet them halfway.

Preventing mistakes:

  • Providing clear, concise, and complete information
  • Make risky, error-prone operations hard to do.
  • Match mental models.
  • Allow people to recover.

Making risky, error-prone operations hard to do

Confirmation overlays are a way to control destructive changes. When a change can't be undone, we ask someone if they're sure. They must only appear when they're necessary. If fewer appear, people will pay more attention to their content.

We noticed that while at events, an error was occurring. When a person presses the sign-out button, a new page appears. There they must select a confirmation sign-out button. People would press the button in the header and walk away from the laptop, assuming they had signed out. Again, it's more about the system and the environment than the person.

So we changed the behaviour of the sign-out button. We don't need a confirmation message for signing out. It's not a destructive action; people can recover since they arrive at the sign-in page.
