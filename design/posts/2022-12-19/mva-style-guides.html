My VAC Account style guides and beta direction

  • To date, My VAC Account has been following the WET base theme for guidance on its look and feel and design patterns.
  • Since then, GCWeb was created and combined WET's toolkit with Canada.ca's design guidance.
    • GCWeb wasn't available to us before WET's update.
  • In 2018-2019, veterans.gc.ca renewed its look and feel, stylizing GCWeb components with VAC colours and fonts.
  • Since then, My VAC Account has been trying to design things with all of these in mind.
  • The beta, for instance, is attempting to weave together WET's base theme, the veterans.gc.ca look and feel, and the Canada.ca style guide (and MVA legacy styles).

Now that the Web Experience Toolkit is updated, we can include the GCWeb (Canada.ca) theme.

Why we should add GCWeb theme and tools to WET:

GCWeb (with Canada.ca's design system) provides user-tested templates, patterns, content guidelines and design principles.

  • Updated theme and tools that are maintained regularly, tested for accessibility, and usability tested
  • Designs consistent with other Canadian institutions builds trust and better user experience
  • New design patterns are released, and we maintain fewer custom patterns: new alerts examples, larger checkboxes/radio buttons examples, and tools like interactive questions 'field-form,' i.e. guided web forms. We can leverage these design patterns without building or maintaining our own
    • There is also templates for server-side functionality available at Centrally Deployed Template Solutions. It templates functionality like signed-in or signed-out headers, reporting problems on a page, and search capability.
  • Veteran Affairs Canada is listed as an institution within the scope of Canada.ca's design requirements.

These organizations must apply Canada.ca design requirements for all their public-facing websites or digital services, regardless of the technology, domain name (both canada.ca and gc.ca) or publishing platform through which these are delivered.

  • https://www.canada.ca/en/government/about/design-system/institutions-list.html
  • https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/government-communications/canada-content-information-architecture-specification/usage-canadaca-design.html
